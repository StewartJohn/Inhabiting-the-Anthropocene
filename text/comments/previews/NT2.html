"Toward a More-Than-Human Anthropocene"

Noah Theriault

By exploring habitability in the Anthropocene, we imply that a human-dominated world can still be a world that supports thriving human populations. This orientation holds a certain appeal, but it is, I contend, in need of serious interrogation. In my previous posts, I have raised the question of who the "anthropos" of the Anthropocene is, and I have explored the pitfalls of various responses to that question.
