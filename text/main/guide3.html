User Guide
Operation Way-finding Posts

The goal of this guide is to help you choose a path through our posts. This page explains features of the posts themselves; the other pages provide an overview of how the site works, and suggest ways to choose which post to start with and which ones to read after that.

A Note About the Posts

The posts appear here substantially as they did on the original blog.

  • Legend. After the main text of each post (but before any comments) is a legend that summarizes the characteristics used to connect the post in the graphs. (As discussed in the Explanation of Emergent Pairings page, the legend can be said to show the post's metadata.) The legend contains (up to) six icons:
    • The icon that represents the post in the graphs.
    • An icon indicating the post type:
      • "Reading Post"--a discussion of a particular reading from the author's home discipline; includes citation information for (and links to) the reading.
      "Reflection Post"--a statement of the author's own views on our topic, often in response to other posts.
    • The icon for the approach associated with the author's home discipline (see explanations available from the Content tab in the main menu at the top of the screen).
    • The icon for the additional approach (if any) found in the post.
    • The icon for a theme addressed in the post, and the outlook taken on it (see explanations available from the Content tab in the main menu at the top of the screen).
    • The icon for a second theme addressed in the post (if present), and the outlook taken on it.
  • Comments. We reproduced any comments a post received on the blog at the end of post as it appears on this site. We do not treat comments as separate posts, with their own nodes.
  • Links.
    • Links to external websites bring up a new browser window.
    • Links to posts in the blog that were not part of the series of posts for this project bring up a new browser window.
    • Links to posts in this series show the post in this space. To return to referring post click its node (highlighted with green border) in the graph--NOTE: depending on your browser, clicking the "back"` button may reset the whole screen.
