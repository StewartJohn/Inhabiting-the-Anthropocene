User Guide
Operation Way-finding Posts

The goal of this site is to help you choose a path through our posts. Before suggesting ways to choose which post to start with, and which ones to read after that, let us give you an overview of how the site works.

Operating the Site

This site is designed to be navigated with the graphs that appear to the left. Each of the four graphs offers a different "view" of the set of posts, by representing different sorts of relationships among them.

  • Use the "Select a View" dropdown menu to the left to activate the corresponding graph.
    • If the graph does not render properly click "Redraw Graph."
    • You can rearrange the nodes by dragging them with the mouse.
    • An explanation of the view will appear in the right of the screen. This will be replaced by the posts (and other content), but you can bring it back by clicking on the name of the view in the bar below the graph, or use the Contents tab in the top menu.
    • A Key to the view will appear below the graph to the left, and will remain until you choose another view.
    • Once in a view you can click the "Contents" tab in the top menu to get to a listing of the posts represented in the graph, and to explanations of other views.
  • In all views, the circles represent posts--color-coded to the author and labeled with his or her initials.
    • Hover the mouse over a post node to see its title, and a bio of the author in the Comments section below the graph.
    • Click on a post node to select it.
      • Post appears at the right of the screen.
      • Author's bio stays in the Comments until the mouse hovers elsewhere.
      • The selected node will have a yellow border when the mouse moves away.
  • In Approaches and Themes views there are additional nodes--colored squares for Approaches, gray polygons for Themes.
    • The Approach colors determine the colors for the authors--see the legend in the Comments section. (It shows unless other information is put there.)
    • Hover and click on these nodes to see explanations--in the Comments, and at the right of the screen.
  • In most views, clicking a node will zoom the graph to a closer look at that node and the others it links to.
    • Click in the white space around the graph to zoom back out.
    • Click on the name of the view below the graph to see the explanation of the view at the right of the screen.
  • Edges (lines between nodes) indicate a relationship between them--depending on the current view.
    • Hover/click to see a brief explanation on the graph, and more detail in the Comments section.
    • In Author-Supplied Pairings View broken/faint edges indicate whether an author identified the linked posts as similar or different. These can be toggled off and on with buttons in the lower right corner of the graph.

Finding your way among the posts

With one exception the graphs do not present determined sequences of posts. They offer multiple paths; the function of the site is to interpret options, in order to inform your choice of what post to read next.

  • Only the Chronological View presents a determined sequence of posts: they are presented in the order they appeared on the original blog. There is no particular order regarding the content--they were posted as fit the authors' schedules.
  • You can certainly read posts in an arbitrary order. Select any view, and simply click.
    • You can use the list of posts available from the Contents tab in the top menu to see titles to help you choose.
    • Or, in any but the Chronological View you can click "Random Post" to have the site choose for you.
  • The Approaches and Themes Views represent the conceptual structure of the relationships among the posts. You can use them to navigate in order to explore different relationships.
    • The Approaches View shows how posts are linked by common intellectual approaches (refer to the Key and hover on approach nodes to see Comments)
      • After reading a post you can read another that takes the same approach.
      • Follow the edge to the approach node you are interested in and click to show other posts related in this way.
    • The Themes View shows how posts are linked by common themes (refer to the Key and hover on approach nodes to see Comments)
      • After reading a post you can read another that addresses the same theme.
      • Follow the edge to the theme node you are interested in and click to show other posts related in this way.
      • NOTE SIGNIFICANCE OF THE STYLE OF THE EDGES.
        • Posts are classed as taking one of two outlooks on the given theme.
        • The outlooks are explained in the pages for the Themes View, and for each theme node--and summarized in the Comments when you hover on and edge.
        • Note the style of the edge that links to the theme you are interesed in--either solid or broken.
        • After you click on that theme, follow the same edge style to read posts that have the same outlook, and the other edge style to read posts that have the contrasting outlook.
  • The Author-Supplied Pairings View shows the authors' suggestions for direct post-to-post connections.
    • For each of their posts the authors suggested two by other authors--one that is broadly similar, one that is broadly different.
      • The edge colors indicate who suggested the pairing; the line style indicates his or her suggestion that the linked posts are similar (broken) or different (faint).
      • (A solid edge indicates that the linked posts are by the same author.)
      • The authors supplied explanations of these pairings, which refer to the approaches, themes and outlooks covered in the posts; these appear in the Comment area when an edge is hovered or clicked.
      • They made some suggestions about the order in which posts should be read--indicated with arrows.
    • To make use of the authors' suggestions
      • Select a starting post--arbitrarily or click Random Post below the graph.
      • When you have read the post, hover over its edges to see suggestions from its author or other authors about companion posts.

A Note About the Posts

The posts appear here substantially as they did on the original blog--with two exceptions.

  • Comments. We reproduced any comments a post received on the blog at the end of post as it appears here. We do not treat comments as separate posts, with their own nodes.
  • Links.
    • Links to external websites appear in a new browser window.
    • Links to posts in the blog that were not part of the series of posts for this project appear in a new browser window.
    • Links to posts in this series do not bring up the post--instead they show the icon for the post so you can select it using a graph.
  • The first eight posts discuss particular readings from the author's home discipline.
    • These "Reading Posts" include citation information for (and links to) the reading, and also suggestions for related readings.
    • These citations, and others mentioned by other posts, are collected on the References page, available from the top menu. [To come]
