User Guide

The goal of this site is for you to read our posts. Before suggesting ways to choose which post to start with, and which ones to read after that, let us give you an overview of how the site operates.

This site is designed to be navigated with the graphs that appear to the left. Each of the four graphs offers a different "view" of the set of posts, by representing different sorts of relationships among them.

  • Use the "Select a View" dropdown menu to the left to activate the corresponding graph.
    • If the graph does not render properly click "Redraw Graph."
    • You can rearrange the nodes by dragging them with the mouse.
    • An explanation of the view will appear in the right of the screen. This will be replaced by the posts (and other content), but you can bring it back by clicking on the name of the view in the bar below the graph[ or use the Contents tap in the top menu].
    • A Key to the view will appear below the graph to the left, and will remain until you choose another view.
    • Once in a view you can click the "Contents" tab in the top menu to see a listing of the posts represented in the graph[ and explanations of other views].
  • In all views, the circles represent posts--color-coded to the author and labeled with his or her initials.
    • Hover the mouse over a post node to see its title, and a bio of the author in the Comments section below the graph.
    • Click on a post node to select it.
      • Post appears at the right of the screen.
      • Author's bio stays in the Comments until the mouse hovers elsewhere.
      • The selected node will have a yellow border when the mouse moves away.
  • In Approaches and Themes views there are additional nodes--colored squares for Approaches, gray polygons for Themes.
    • The Approach colors determine the colors for the authors--see the legend in the Comments section. (It shows unless other information is put there.)
    • Hover and click on these nodes to see explanations--in the Comments, and at the right of the screen.
  • In most views, clicking a node will zoom the graph to a closer look at that node and the others it links to.
    • Click in the white space around the graph to zoom back out.
    • Click on the name of the view below the graph to see the explanation of the view at the right of the screen.
  • Edges (lines between nodes) indicate a relationship between them--depending on the current view.
    • Hover/click to see a brief explanation on the graph, and more detail in the Comments section.
    • In Author-Supplied Pairings View broken/faint edges indicate whether an author identified the linked posts as similar or different. These can be toggled off and on with buttons in the lower right corner of the graph.

the Approaches and Themes vie

You choose which graph to activate with the . When you select a view a more detailed explanation of it appears in this window, and a brief explanation appears below the graph, to the left.

  • Approaches View. This shows nodes for the posts and for the four approaches taken the authors, and edges showing which posts take which approach. This table explains how to operate this view:
        Post Node Approach
      Node      		 Edge
      Hover Shows title; author bio in Comments Shows title; brief explanation in Comments Shows title; brief explanation in Comments
      Click Post shows in this window; bio remains until mouse hovers elsewhere in graph Long explanation shows in this window; bio remains until mouse hovers elsewhere in graph  
  • Hovering the mouse over a post node shows its title and the author's photo and bio in the Comments area below the graph to the right. Clicking brings up the post in this window, and makes the bio stay until the mouse hovers elsewhere in the graph. This functionality is the same in all four views.
  • Hovering above an approach node shows an explanation the Comments area; clicking will keep it there until the mouse hovers elsewhere in the graph.
  • Hovering over edges shows a brief explanation of why the linked post is associated with that approach (either due to the author's home discipline, or because it appears in the post).
  • By default the Comments section explains the color scheme for the post nodes.

Themes View. This shows nodes for the posts and also for the four themes explored in the posts. Hovering the mouse above the theme nodes shows an explanation in the Comments area below the graph to the right.
